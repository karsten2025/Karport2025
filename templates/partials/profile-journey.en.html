Professional Journey

My career path is marked by a continuous evolution from a technical expert to a strategic architect of complex transformation processes. For over two decades, I have learned how to achieve sustainable results in demanding industrial environments by aligning technology, processes, and people.

Project Manager, German Steel Manufacturer (2007 – Present)

In my long-standing role as a Project Manager for a leading German steel producer, I am in the role of a servant in complex sub-projects within large-scale transformation initiatives. My focus is on realizing strategic objectives by implementing infrastructure, environmental, and efficiency projects—from modernizing production facilities to meet environmental standards to introducing automated logistics systems for significant cost reduction.

Operations Representative & Project Engineer (2005 – 2007)

As Operations Representative in special engineering, I led business process transformations and implemented agile methods for value analysis. Here, I built the crucial bridge between operational excellence and strategic realignment.

Quality and Project Engineer (2002 – 2005)

In this role, I established integrated quality and project management models according to ISO 9001/16949. Direct customer consulting and representing the company in technical committees sharpened my ability to translate technical requirements into marketable solutions.

Technical Foundation (1994 – 2002)

My career began with a solid technical education as a state-certified machine technician and later as a degreed engineer (Dipl.-Ing. FH) in Mechanical Engineering. This hands-on experience in CNC, lean management, and quality assurance continues to form the resilient foundation for my management decisions today.
